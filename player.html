LIONEL MESSI

Born in Rosario, Argentina, Messi grew up in a working-class family and began playing football at a very young age, coached by his father. At age eight, he joined Newell's Old Boys' youth system. However, his development was threatened when he was diagnosed with a growth hormone deficiency. Local clubs in Argentina could not afford the expensive treatments. In 2000, at age 13, Messi relocated to Spain with his family to try out for FC Barcelona, who agreed to pay for his medical treatment and enrolled him in their famed La Masia youth academy.

    Lionel Messi is currently captaining the Argentina national football team at the 2026 FIFA World Cup, where he just guided his country into a second consecutive World Cup final following a dramatic 2-1 semifinal victory over England. At 39 years old, Messi continues to dominate at the absolute highest level, splitting his time between international duty and his club career with Major League Soccer (MLS) side Inter Miami

    Historic Records:

    By taking the pitch this summer, Messi became the first male player in football history to feature in six separate World Cups. He also broke the record for the most knockout stage assists in World Cup history

FC Barcelona

Lionel Messi’s journey with FC Barcelona is the most successful player-club relationship in football history, spanning 21 years from a teenage prodigy to an international icon. Arriving at the club's La Masia academy as a 13-year-old from Rosario, Argentina, in 2000, Messi went on to break nearly every major record in European club football before his emotional departure due to financial constraints in August 2021

Paris Saint-Germain

Lionel Messi spent two seasons at Paris Saint-Germain (PSG) between August 2021 and June 2023, arriving as a free agent from FC Barcelona and registering 32 goals and 34 assists in 75 total appearances. During his tenure at the Parc des Princes, the Argentine icon helped the capital club capture two consecutive Ligue 1 titles and the 2022 Trophée des Champions before departing for MLS side Inter Miami

Inter Miami

Lionel Messi is the captain and focal point of Inter Miami CF, having already guided the club to its first-ever MLS Cup title in December 2025. The 39-year-old superstar is currently in spectacular form for both club and country during the ongoing 2026 season

First worldcup of Lionel Messi

Lionel Messi's first World Cup was the 2006 FIFA World Cup in Germany, where he burst onto the international stage as an 18-year-old prodigy. Wearing the number 19 shirt, he became the youngest player to ever represent Argentina at a World Cup when he made his historic debut.

Second worldcup of Lionel Messi

Lionel Messi's second World Cup was the 2010 FIFA World Cup in South Africa, where he played a pivotal role for Argentina. Wearing the iconic number 10 jersey, Messi showcased his exceptional skills and creativity on the global stage.

Third worldcup of Lionel Messi

Lionel Messi's third World Cup was the 2014 FIFA World Cup in Brazil, where he led Argentina to the final and won the Golden Ball award as the tournament's best player. Messi's exceptional performances throughout the competition solidified his status as one of the greatest footballers of all time.
